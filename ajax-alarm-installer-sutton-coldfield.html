Why Sutton Coldfield homes choose us

What a typical Sutton Coldfield install looks like

Most of the homes we work on across the Royal Town fall into one of three shapes. Each gets a slightly different configuration, but the principles are the same: cover every entry point, sensible motion detection inside, and a siren that’s loud enough to deter without annoying the neighbours.

  • Three-bed semis around Boldmere and Wylde Green: Hub plus front and back door contacts, two motion sensors (hallway and landing), and an external siren. Typically a 3-hour install.
  • Four-bed detached homes around Mere Green and Four Oaks: Hub plus four to six door/window contacts, three to four motion sensors, and a high-output external siren. Often includes a garage sensor.
  • Larger properties around Little Aston or Moor Hall: Multi-room sensor setups, additional sirens, and Ajax cameras with photo verification.

Every survey is free and there’s no obligation. We’ll walk through your property, explain where sensors should go and why, and give you a clear written quote, with the price you pay being the price we quote.

About the Royal Town

Sutton Coldfield gained its royal status in 1528 when King Henry VIII granted it a Charter of Incorporation. Today it’s home to around 95,000 residents, world-class green space at Sutton Park, one of the largest urban parks in Europe, and some of the West Midlands’ most sought-after schools. It’s our patch and we’re proud to keep its homes safe.
